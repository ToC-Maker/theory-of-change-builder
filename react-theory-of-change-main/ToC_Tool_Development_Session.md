## 9. Chain Health Check

**Co-Pilot (9a):** Let's multiply probabilities from inputs to End Goal:

**Core chain probability**: 80% × 60% × 75% × 50% × 70% = **12.6%**

🔴 This puts you in the low confidence range (<25%).

The math suggests that for every organization that discovers your tool, roughly 1 in 8 will ultimately achieve the full End Goal of strategic clarity and appropriate funding access. However, this doesn't account for volume - if many organizations use the tool, even 12.6% success could be significant impact.

**Options:**
1. Strengthen weak links (funder recognition at 50% is the weakest)
2. Split the chain (track intermediate successes separately)  
3. Accept the risk (12.6% might be acceptable given high potential volume and impact)

**Cian:** I think this is actually reasonable. We're trying to change both organizational behavior AND funder behavior, which is inherently difficult. If we can get hundreds of organizations using the tool, 12-13% achieving full strategic clarity and funding access would be a major success. Plus many organizations will benefit from intermediate outcomes even if they don't reach the end goal.

**Co-Pilot (9b):** Good perspective. The low probability is offset by high potential volume and significant intermediate value. 12.6% overall probability accepted. 🔴 Low confidence level acknowledged.

**Cian:** Confirmed.

**End Mission**: More effective allocation of charitable resources globally and expanded effective giving ecosystem
