# CEEALAR (EA Hotel) — Research Brief

## 1) Quick overview

* **Name:** Centre for Enabling EA Learning & Research (CEEALAR); formerly “EA Hotel.” Rebranded in Jan 2020. ([Effective Altruism Forum][1])
* **What it is:** A Blackpool-based charity that offers free or subsidized accommodation, meals, and community so people can focus on EA-relevant learning, research, and entrepreneurial projects. ([ceealar.org][2])
* **Legal status:** Registered charity (CIO) in England & Wales, **Charity no. 1189768**; public filings/financials available at the Charity Commission. ([Charity Register][3])
* **Location:** Athene Hotel, 36 York Street, Blackpool, UK (original site acquired 2018). ([Wikipedia][4])

---

## 2) Mission, model & activities

**Mission (site language):** Provide “catered accommodation so \[grantees] can focus full-time on EA work without financial pressure,” targeting people earlier in their “impact journey.” ([ceealar.org][2])

**Model:**

* Hosts accepted **grantees** (subsidized; sometimes fully covered) and **patrons** (paying) typically in 90-day blocks with possible extensions up to 12 months. ([ceealar.org][5])
* Historically emphasized cost-effectiveness vs. traditional grants: earlier figures suggested ~~**£6,000 per person-year** all-in (2019 pitch) and \*\*~~£800/month\*\* (2022 update). These are historical benchmarks; current costs may differ. ([Effective Altruism Forum][6])
* Provides coworking, catered dinners (often vegan), and a peer community; runs/hosts events, retreats, and small fellowships. ([ceealar.org][7])

---

## 3) History & milestones

* **2018:** Founder Greg Colbourn purchases a 17-room hotel in Blackpool (financed from prior crypto gains) to pilot a low-cost EA hub. ([Wikipedia][4])
* **2018–2019:** Early “EA Hotel” fundraiser series and outputs posts on the EA Forum; significant community discussion and media attention. ([ea.greaterwrong.com][8])
* **Jan 2020:** Rebrands to CEEALAR; begins UK charity registration. ([Effective Altruism Forum][1])
* **June 2020:** Registered as Charity 1189768 (CIO). ([Charity Register][3])
* **2023–2024:** Funding-constrained → runway stabilized via community donors and institutional grants (SFF 2023; AISTO & EAIF 2024, per Manifund project page). ([Effective Altruism Forum][9], [manifund.org][10])

## 4) Impact & outputs

**Selected metrics (site/ToC pages):**

* **20,900+ person-days** of work done at CEEALAR (cumulative).
* **Avg. 12 new meaningful connections** per grantee; **\~80%** report being more productive while at CEEALAR.
* **Incubated/supported**: Orxl, Convergence Analysis, AI Standards Lab, ML4G, Electric Sheep, among others.
  (These figures are self-reported by CEEALAR; see site for detail.) ([ceealar.org][11])

* **Stays:** Apply as **Grantee** or **Patron** for up to **90 days**, extendable (up to 12 months). Selection for grantees weighs proposed work and **counterfactual impact**. Some grantees may be eligible for additional financial support. ([ceealar.org][5])

## 9) Notable themes / discussion points

* **Cost-effective incubation:** Low-cost location (Blackpool) and shared services enable unusually cheap exploratory research/learning time for early-career people. ([Effective Altruism Forum][6])
* **Funding cyclicality:** Periodic runway crunches; increased diversification in 2023–2024 with SFF/AISTO/EAIF and alumni/community donors. ([Effective Altruism Forum][9], [manifund.org][10])
* **Impact measurement:** CEEALAR tracks person-days, self-reported productivity, network connections, and downstream placements/incubations; these are partly qualitative or self-reported and should be interpreted accordingly. ([ceealar.org][11])
* **Governance transparency:** Annual accounts, trustee lists, and notes are publicly filed and worth reviewing for risk, reserves, and related-party items (e.g., loan account). ([Charity Register][3])
