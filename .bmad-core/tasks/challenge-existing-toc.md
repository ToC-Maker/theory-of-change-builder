# Challenge Existing Theory of Change Task

## Task Overview
Conduct a comprehensive adversarial analysis of an existing Theory of Change document, challenging every assumption, logical connection, and claimed outcome with extreme skepticism.

## Prerequisites
- Existing Theory of Change document has been provided
- Agent is in highly critical/skeptical mode
- Adversarial analysis framework is activated

## Process Steps

### Step 1: Initial Assessment
1. Read the entire Theory of Change document thoroughly
2. Identify the overall structure and logic model
3. Extract all key components:
   - End goals and ultimate outcomes
   - Causal chains and logical connections
   - Key assumptions and dependencies
   - Success metrics and indicators
   - Resource requirements and timelines

### Step 2: Systematic Deconstruction
Apply adversarial analysis to each component:

**Goal Analysis:**
- Challenge the specificity and measurability of stated goals
- Question whether the goals are actually valuable or necessary
- Demand evidence that achieving these goals matters
- Explore alternative goals that might be more important

**Causal Chain Challenge:**
- Question every arrow and connection in the logic model
- Demand evidence for each claimed causal relationship
- Identify missing steps or hidden assumptions in the chains
- Challenge the strength and reliability of causal connections

**Assumption Interrogation:**
- Extract all unstated assumptions underlying the theory
- Challenge each assumption with hostile questioning
- Explore scenarios where key assumptions fail
- Demand evidence supporting critical assumptions

**Evidence Scrutiny:**
- Challenge all cited evidence for quality and relevance
- Question research methodology and sample sizes
- Identify cherry-picked data or confirmation bias
- Demand additional evidence for weak claims

### Step 3: Alternative Scenarios
Generate challenging alternative scenarios:
- What if the intervention has no effect?
- What if the intervention causes harm?
- What if resources were allocated differently?
- What if the problem solves itself without intervention?
- What if there are better approaches not considered?

### Step 4: Implementation Reality Check
Challenge practical feasibility:
- Question resource requirements and availability
- Challenge timeline assumptions and dependencies
- Identify potential implementation failures
- Examine organizational capacity to execute
- Explore political and contextual barriers

### Step 5: Stakeholder Opposition Analysis
Consider resistance and pushback:
- Who might oppose this theory or intervention?
- What are their legitimate concerns?
- How might stakeholders game or subvert the system?
- What unintended consequences might emerge?

## Conversation Format
Structure as adversarial dialogue:
```
**Dr. Sarah Chen (Skeptical Analyst):** [Direct challenge with specific examples and demanding questions]

**Theory Defender:** [Attempted defense with evidence and reasoning]

**Dr. Sarah Chen:** [Even more pointed follow-up, attacking weak points and demanding stronger evidence]

**Theory Defender:** [Revised response with additional support]

[Continue this pattern for 10-15 exchanges per major component]
```

## Success Criteria
- Every major component of the theory has been challenged
- Challenges are substantive and well-reasoned (not trivial)
- Alternative scenarios are explored comprehensively
- Evidence quality is rigorously examined
- Implementation feasibility is questioned
- Document length: 10,000+ words minimum
- Challenges lead to actionable improvements

## Quality Checks
- Are the challenges genuinely difficult to address?
- Have all major assumptions been identified and questioned?
- Is the evidence scrutiny thorough and fair?
- Are alternative scenarios realistic and challenging?
- Does the analysis improve the original theory's robustness?
