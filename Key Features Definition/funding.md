Title
Impact Map: Build Your Theory of Change
Project in a tweet
AI-powered interactive planner that turns big goals into testable Theories of Change with website-ready flowcharts.
Intro
Even well-resourced Effective-Altruism projects stumble because their Theory of Change (ToC) lives only in founders’ heads. After spotting this gap, our lead Zoé won a ClearerThinking micro-grant and prototyped a guided ToC builder. When no-code tools proved too rigid for AI-assisted critique, she migrated to an open-source stack and recruited volunteer engineers. Thirty-plus practitioners—from AI-safety teams to global-health NGOs—have already signed up to beta-test the new version.
Alignment with Cosmos × FIRE truth-seeking principles
Ask before you answer. Sessions start by eliciting the user’s goal and first-pass causal story before any autocompletion.


Test with opposition. A built-in Assumption Challenger applies adversarial evidence, reference-class data, and LLM or human peer critique to develop critical assumption tests.


Trace the reasoning. Each edit updates a version-controlled Mermaid diagram and an exportable JSON ledger, all MIT-licensed for audit and remix—guarding against “black-box convenience.”
Problem
Interviews with EA practitioners show most projects rely on vague or implicit ToCs, wasting talent and money. Over 30 project leaders and EA community members showed interest in a ToC Buider tool and signed up to beta test the new version. 
Medium
Web app with a visual flow-chart builder, LLM-powered assumption challenger, experiment generator, probabilistic path explorer, and progress tracker.
Execution plan (90 days)
Wks 1-2 Refine prototype, integrate visuals & critic prompts
Wks 3-4 Ship MVP: goal→outcome builder, assumption rating, experiment suggestions
Wks 5-8 Beta with 15 users; A/B-test prompts; iterate weekly
Wks 9-12 Public release, docs, template library
Success case
Pilot (90 days)
≥70 % users report +1 clarity (5-point scale)
≥ 70 % of critical assumptions have an explicit test plan
≥ 60 % launch at least one critical assumption test
Six-month horizon
≥30 % monthly active users (of accounts created)
≥ 5 published (e.g., on EA org websites) or grant-submitted ToCs that used the tool
≥ 3 public case studies demonstrating measurable impact improvements (e.g., resources re-allocated, funding won, time saved)
When reasoning is legible, contestable, and continuously tested, resources flow toward what works—and the rest can be peaceably discarded.
