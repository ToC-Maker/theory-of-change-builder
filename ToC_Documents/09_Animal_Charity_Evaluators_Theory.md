# Animal Charity Evaluators Theory of Change Framework - Full Content

## Executive Summary
Animal Charity Evaluators (ACE) develops comprehensive frameworks for evaluating the effectiveness of animal advocacy interventions. Their meta-level approach aims to maximize impact for animals by identifying the most promising approaches, supporting effective organizations, and building the capacity of the broader animal advocacy movement through research-driven recommendations.

## Theory of Change Framework

### Inputs (Resources & Activities)
- **Research Infrastructure**: Team of researchers, analysts, and evaluation specialists
- **Evaluation Methodology**: Rigorous frameworks for assessing intervention effectiveness and organizational capacity
- **Data Collection Systems**: Methods for gathering evidence on animal advocacy outcomes and organizational performance
- **Expert Networks**: Relationships with researchers, advocates, and practitioners across animal welfare domains
- **Funding for Operations**: Resources to conduct evaluations, publish research, and maintain organizational capacity
- **Technology Platforms**: Systems for data analysis, report publication, and donor engagement
- **Movement Intelligence**: Ongoing monitoring of trends, opportunities, and challenges in animal advocacy

### Outputs (Direct Deliverables)
- **Top Charity Recommendations**: Annual identification of most effective animal advocacy organizations
- **Research Publications**: In-depth reports on intervention effectiveness, movement trends, and strategic insights
- **Evaluation Frameworks**: Standardized methodologies for assessing animal advocacy approaches
- **Donor Guidance**: Resources helping funders make evidence-based giving decisions
- **Movement Analysis**: Strategic assessments of promising areas for advocacy focus
- **Organizational Reviews**: Detailed evaluations of charity programs, capacity, and impact potential
- **Intervention Research**: Analysis of specific advocacy tactics and their comparative effectiveness

### Outcomes (System Changes)
- **Improved Resource Allocation**: More funding directed toward highest-impact animal advocacy interventions
- **Enhanced Advocacy Effectiveness**: Organizations adopting more evidence-based approaches to their work
- **Movement Professionalization**: Higher standards for evaluation and impact measurement across animal advocacy
- **Strategic Focus**: Movement coordination around most promising intervention areas
- **Donor Education**: Increased sophistication in animal advocacy funding decisions
- **Research Culture**: Greater emphasis on evidence-based decision making within animal advocacy
- **Organizational Development**: Improved capacity and effectiveness of top-performing animal advocacy organizations

### Ultimate Goal
Maximize the reduction of animal suffering by ensuring resources flow to the most effective interventions and organizations, while building a more strategic, evidence-based, and impactful animal advocacy movement.

## Strategic Approach

### Evaluation Framework Components

#### 1. Intervention Assessment
- **Impact Potential**: Analysis of how much suffering an intervention could prevent or alleviate
- **Evidence Base**: Evaluation of research supporting intervention effectiveness
- **Tractability**: Assessment of how solvable the targeted problem appears to be
- **Neglectedness**: Analysis of whether the intervention area receives adequate attention and resources

#### 2. Organizational Evaluation
- **Program Effectiveness**: Assessment of how well organizations execute their chosen interventions
- **Room for More Funding**: Analysis of organizations' capacity to effectively use additional resources
- **Track Record**: Evaluation of past performance and organizational learning
- **Leadership and Strategy**: Assessment of organizational vision, planning, and execution capacity

#### 3. Movement Building Analysis
- **Talent Pipeline**: Assessment of human capital development within animal advocacy
- **Research Gaps**: Identification of areas where additional evidence is needed
- **Strategic Coordination**: Analysis of opportunities for improved movement collaboration
- **Cultural and Political Context**: Understanding of factors affecting advocacy success

### Key Focus Areas

#### 1. Factory Farming Interventions
- **Corporate Campaigns**: Effectiveness of cage-free and welfare reform initiatives
- **Plant-Based and Cultivated Alternatives**: Market-based approaches to reducing animal product consumption
- **Policy and Legal Reform**: Legislative and regulatory approaches to improving farm animal welfare
- **Consumer Education**: Direct advocacy to reduce animal product consumption

#### 2. Wild Animal Welfare
- **Research and Capacity Building**: Developing the scientific and advocacy infrastructure for wild animal interventions
- **Intervention Development**: Identifying specific, tractable approaches to reducing wild animal suffering
- **Movement Building**: Growing awareness and capacity for wild animal advocacy

#### 3. Movement Meta-Work
- **Research and Development**: Building the evidence base for animal advocacy effectiveness
- **Talent Development**: Programs to attract and develop effective animal advocates
- **Strategic Coordination**: Facilitating collaboration and resource allocation across the movement

### Implementation Strategy

#### Phase 1: Research and Analysis
- Comprehensive literature reviews and intervention mapping
- Development of evaluation criteria and assessment frameworks
- Initial charity evaluations and recommendation development

#### Phase 2: Recommendation and Outreach
- Publication of charity recommendations and research findings
- Engagement with donors, advocates, and organizations to promote evidence-based approaches
- Building feedback loops to improve evaluation methodology

#### Phase 3: Movement Development
- Long-term capacity building within animal advocacy
- Development of research infrastructure and evaluation culture
- Strategic coordination to address key movement gaps and opportunities

### Impact Measurement
- **Funding Influenced**: Tracking dollars directed to recommended organizations and approaches
- **Organizational Improvements**: Measuring capacity and effectiveness increases among evaluated organizations
- **Movement Adoption**: Assessment of evidence-based practice adoption across animal advocacy
- **Research Contribution**: Evaluation of how ACE research influences broader movement strategy

This theory of change emphasizes the power of rigorous evaluation and strategic analysis to transform animal advocacy from intuition-driven to evidence-based, ultimately maximizing impact for the billions of animals affected by human activities.
