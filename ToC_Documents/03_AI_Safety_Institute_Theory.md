# AI Safety Institute International Network Theory of Change - Full Content

## Executive Summary
The AI Safety Institute International Network, launched at the AI Seoul Summit in May 2024, represents a coordinated international effort to develop safety standards and governance frameworks for frontier AI systems. The network includes the United States, United Kingdom, European Union, Japan, Singapore, South Korea, Canada, France, Kenya, and Australia.

## Theory of Change Framework

### Inputs (Resources & Activities)
- **Government Resources**: National funding for AI safety research and regulatory capacity
- **International Cooperation**: Diplomatic coordination between network member countries
- **Technical Expertise**: AI safety researchers, policy experts, industry specialists from member nations
- **Regulatory Authority**: Government power to implement and enforce AI safety standards
- **Research Infrastructure**: Testing facilities, evaluation frameworks, safety assessment capabilities
- **Industry Engagement**: Collaboration with AI companies and frontier AI developers
- **Multi-stakeholder Participation**: Civil society, academia, and private sector involvement

### Outputs (Direct Deliverables)
- **Frontier AI Safety Commitments**: Binding agreements from AI companies on safety practices
- **International Safety Standards**: Coordinated frameworks for evaluating and managing AI risks
- **Testing and Evaluation Protocols**: Standardized methods for assessing frontier AI capabilities and risks
- **Safety Framework Publications**: Public documentation of safety requirements and best practices
- **Policy Recommendations**: Guidelines for national AI governance and regulation
- **Research Collaborations**: Joint safety research initiatives between member countries
- **Capability Assessment Tools**: Methods for identifying when AI systems pose severe risks

### Outcomes (System Changes)
- **Coordinated Global Governance**: Harmonized AI safety approaches across major AI-developing nations
- **Industry Safety Adoption**: AI companies proactively implement safety measures to meet international standards
- **Regulatory Preparedness**: National governments equipped with frameworks to govern advanced AI
- **Risk Mitigation**: Reduced likelihood of catastrophic outcomes from frontier AI development
- **International Accountability**: Transparent reporting and monitoring of AI safety practices globally
- **Research Acceleration**: Faster progress on AI safety science through coordinated international effort
- **Public Trust**: Increased confidence in AI development through transparent safety governance

### Ultimate Goal
Ensure the safe, secure, and trustworthy development and deployment of frontier AI systems globally through coordinated international governance, preventing catastrophic risks while enabling beneficial AI applications.

## Strategic Approach

### Key Focus Areas:

#### 1. Frontier AI Risk Assessment
- **Severe Risk Identification**: Developing frameworks to identify AI systems that could pose catastrophic risks
- **Capability Thresholds**: Establishing clear benchmarks for when enhanced safety measures are required
- **Risk Categorization**: Systematic classification of AI risks across security, safety, and societal domains

#### 2. International Coordination Mechanisms
- **Network Governance**: Establishing goals, collaboration mechanisms, and strategic coordination
- **Information Sharing**: Protocols for sharing safety research and risk assessments between member countries
- **Policy Harmonization**: Aligning national AI policies with international safety standards

#### 3. Industry Engagement and Compliance
- **Voluntary Commitments**: Securing proactive safety pledges from leading AI developers
- **Transparency Requirements**: Mandating disclosure of safety practices and risk assessments
- **Accountability Mechanisms**: Systems for monitoring and enforcing safety commitments

### Implementation Framework:

#### Phase 1: Network Establishment (2024)
- Formation of international network with founding members
- Development of shared principles and objectives
- Initial safety commitment agreements from AI companies

#### Phase 2: Standard Development (2024-2025)
- Creation of technical safety standards and evaluation protocols
- Establishment of testing frameworks and capability assessments
- Development of governance mechanisms and accountability systems

#### Phase 3: Implementation and Enforcement (2025+)
- Deployment of safety standards across member countries
- Monitoring and evaluation of AI company compliance
- Continuous improvement of standards based on technological developments

### Core Principles:
- **Responsible Development**: AI systems should be developed and deployed responsibly with appropriate safety measures
- **Transparency**: Clear communication about AI capabilities, limitations, and safety practices
- **International Cooperation**: Coordinated approach to managing global AI risks
- **Adaptability**: Frameworks that can evolve with rapidly advancing AI technology

This theory of change emphasizes the critical need for international coordination in AI governance, recognizing that frontier AI development is a global challenge requiring collaborative solutions to ensure beneficial outcomes for all humanity.
